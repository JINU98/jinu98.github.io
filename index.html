About Me

I am a PhD student in Computer Engineering at the University of South Carolina, working in the ICAS Lab with Prof. Ramtin Zand. My research focuses on making Large Language Models (LLMs) efficient and deployable on resource-constrained hardware.

I work at the intersection of quantization, processing-in-memory (PIM), and heterogeneous hardware (edge TPUs, GPUs, and custom accelerators) to enable real-time, on-device LLMs. I also collaborate on applied AI systems in mental health (WellDunn, CPR), healthcare (MDiabates), and disaster response (social media‚Äìdriven dashboards).
